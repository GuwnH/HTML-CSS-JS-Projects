Frankie "The Answer" Edgar

A Collection Of His Greatest Achievements:

  • February 3, 2007: Got out of a deep kneebar from Tyson Griffin and won his UFC Debut through unanimous decision
  • April 10, 2010: Won the UFC Lightweight Belt by beating BJ Penn
  • January 1, 2011: Went to war with Gray Maynard and secured a decision after getting dropped several times within multiple rounds
  • October 8, 2011: Rematched Gray Maynard and got dropped multiple times but survived long enough to win by KO and end the trilogy.
  • February 2, 2013: Made a move down to featherweight and went to decision with an all-time great in Jose Aldo
  • July 6, 2013: Defeated a newcomer prospect in Charles Olivera (who would later be an incredible champion).
  • July 6, 2013: Koed a legend in Chad Mendes to top off his 4 fight win streak in featherweight.
  • May 13, 2017: Snapped the 8-fight win-streak of rising contender Yair Rodriguez.
  • August 22, 2020: Made a move down to Bantamweight and defeated rank 8 Pedro Munchoz at age 39.
  • November 12, 2022: Retired after a 3 fight loss-skid all by knock outs

Frankie Edgar is an underrated fighter only known by hardcore MMA fans. During his lightweight career, he was vastly undersized and stated that he did not cut weight. Meaning that his weigh in weight was the same as his cage weight and he was fighting people who were likely 170-185lbs or 15-30lbs heavier than him. He was also 5'6 and the shortest man in the division and easily the smallest overall. Yet despite this, he was able to win fights against larger legends and become a champion. It wasn't until he was past his prime that he was fighting in weight classes that he wasn't undersized in.
